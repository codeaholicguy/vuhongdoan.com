Klarna - Desktop Store App

Helping retailers to drive sales and deliver exceptional digital customer experiences through live chat and video calls.

Platform
Desktop Web App (B2B)
Year
2023
Team
PM, Engineers, Content Designer, Product Designer

My Role
Sole Product Designer
Background
The Klarna Store App has a proven track record of helping merchants increase their online conversion and average order value. Initially, the app was designed to enable in-store staff to chat with online shoppers via their mobile devices, operating under the assumption that all users were based in physical stores where mobile use is more convenient.

However, our conversations with merchants revealed a broader range of needs. Many retailers either do not have physical stores or their sales associates work from back offices, necessitating the availability of the app on desktops as well. Even those in physical stores expressed a preference for the option to engage with shoppers from their desktops, rather than relying solely on their mobile devices.

Recognizing this opportunity, we decided to expand our offering and enhance our competitiveness by leveraging our existing technology to develop a desktop app.
Early insights
In conversations with our merchants, we identified several key obstacles they faced with our existing product offerings.
Users have to switch between devices
Merchants use multiple systems for customer engagement and data collection including our mobile app and a desktop solution (such as Zendesk).

This setup forced users to switch between devices constantly, disrupting their workflow and reducing efficiency.
Perception of unprofessionalism when using mobile phone in-store
In-store customers perceived sales associates as unprofessional when they were seen using their phones, even though they were engaged in online customer chats.

This negative perception affected the store’s image and customer satisfaction.
Trust and monitoring issues
Store managers struggled to know whether sales associates were genuinely assisting online customers on their phones or were distracted with non-work activities.

This uncertainty damaged the trust between store managers and their staff, resulting in worries about efficiency and responsibility.
Merchants churn due to competitor advantage
Some merchants chose to sign agreements with competitors who offered more efficient desktop versions of similar products.

The lack of a desktop solution was a significant factor in their decision to switch, highlighting the need for a desktop app to retain and attract merchants.
These insights highlighted the necessity of developing a desktop version of the Klarna Store App to provide a more efficient, cohesive, and competitive solution for our merchants.
My role
My main role involved crafting the end-to-end user experience for the desktop app.

Being the only product designer for the project, my responsibility is to ensure a seamless user experience across different devices. I led efforts to enhance the overall user experience and tackle user difficulties associated with the Store App.
From Concept to Execution
I started mapping out our product ecosystem to understand where and how the desktop app could fit in. This comprehensive overview allowed us to identify potential integration points and ensure a cohesive user experience across platforms.​​​​​​​
Collaborating with stakeholders, the product manager, and engineers, and analyzing merchant feedback, we identified the features that would provide the most value and drive adoption to build for the first release including end-to-end experience in chats management.

These sessions were crucial in aligning our priorities and ensuring the new desktop app met our users' needs effectively.
✨  Spotlight features
There are several features that were included in this project. In this case study, I will highlight the two most important features implemented to demonstrate how users start chatting with their online customers. These features are essential to our product offerings and were frequently used on our mobile app.
1. Claiming a Chat 💬
This feature empowers users to efficiently manage incoming chats by allowing them to claim a chat from an online shopper. When a customer initiates a conversation, it appears in a queue visible to all available sales associates.
Research findings 
I started by analyzing the current experience on our existing mobile app and talking with our users to gain a deeper understanding of their workflows.

Existing Mobile App Design - Claim a Chat

As the mobile app was designed a few years ago, I have identified several key areas that can be easily addressed with the new desktop app.
The full-screen overlay causes disruptions
While it effectively grabs users' attention, it can also interrupt their app experience. This is particularly frustrating for users who are in the middle of a conversation with another customer, causing unnecessary disruptions and inefficiencies.
Users struggle to know how much time they have left
In busy situations, users might prefer to wait until the last few seconds to see if no one else claims the chat, ensuring that the customer still receives support. This can lead to missed opportunities and decreased efficiency.
Difficult for users to identify the chat just claimed
The chat request doesn't display the new customer's name, making it difficult for users to quickly locate and identify the conversation they just claimed. This can cause confusion and delays in responding.
Design and Iterations
Collaborating closely with a content designer, I explored various design options for this feature and then shared them with the team to collect feedback.

I have discovered that displaying the claiming chat modal as a small banner at the bottom-left corner of large screens can be beneficial for users. This placement takes advantage of the extra space available and ensures that it doesn't disrupt their current activities.

Due to the time-sensitive nature of this modal, I collaborated closely with engineers to enhance the notification experience. This involved implementing sounds and push notifications to ensure they are easily noticeable, even when users are not actively viewing the chat screens.

After a few rounds of iteration, the final design was decided as below:
If users miss the chat...
After carefully evaluating the current logic of chat routing, with the help of engineers, I decided to apply the new UI without making any changes to the existing flow. I found no issues with the current setup and determined that modifications were unnecessary.

This process of chat routing ensures that the customer is connected with an associate as quickly as possible, reducing wait times and enhancing the overall customer experience
2. Chat Transfer 🔀
Chat Transfer was designed to facilitate seamless communication between users and their colleagues. It enables users to seamlessly hand over the conversation to an available colleague, preventing customer wait times and ensuring uninterrupted support.
Research findings 
Based on conversations with users and a thorough analysis of the current mobile app experience, I have identified several key areas with the current design that should be addressed:

Existing Mobile App Design - Chat Transfer

Full-screen overlay hinders users from multitasking
While waiting for a response, it is important for users to continue providing support to other customers. This limitation reduces their overall productivity and responsiveness.
Difficulty in finding specific team members
This becomes problematic when a customer requests to chat with a particular associate. This inefficiency can lead to frustration for both the customer and the sales associate.
Sending requests to the entire team can be annoying
In situations where users need to transfer a chat to the entire team or any available colleague due to unexpected reasons, the current design necessitates the manual selection of one-by-one team members. It can be overwhelming, particularly when users are feeling the pressure to consistently offer support.
Design and Iterations
I started exploring various design options for this feature with the following design goals in mind:
•   Flexibility: Give users the ability to multitask while the transfer is in progress
•   Efficiency: Enhance the UI to help users easily and quickly complete their task
Option #1: Pop-over modal
✔️ Pros:
•   Users can multitask when the transfer is in progress
❌ Cons:
•   The pop-over modal blocks users from interacting with the rest of the screen. It's crucial when they have an incoming chat request that they need to take action in a short time.

•   If users navigate to other pages while the transfer request is in progress, they will be unaware of the status of their request as it's hidden in a certain chat. It's also hard to remember which chat they are trying to transfer.
Option #2: Embedded modal
✔️ Pros:
•   Users can multitask when the transfer is in progress
•   Users can continue interacting with other areas of the screen as necessary
❌ Cons:
•   Similar to option 1, users will only be able to know the status of their request if they are in this chat.
•   The Shopper profile view has been minimized when the Transfer modal opens. It can be annoying when they have to reference information from the Shopper profile in their transfer note.
- Final Design -
Option #3: Floating modal
✔️ Pros:
•   Users can multitask when the transfer is in progress
•   Users can continue interacting with other areas of the screen as necessary
•   Users have the flexibility to move the modal to a location that suits their needs
❌ Cons:
•   The view can become cluttered when multiple transfer requests are in progress.​​​​​​​ However, we can see clearly from the data that users rarely send out multiple transfer requests simultaneously. That's why after careful consideration, we decided to go with this option, as its drawback is acceptable.​​​​​​​
❇️ Outcomes
This product significantly enhanced merchant operations and experiences, resulting in:

•   Improved conversion rates by 7%; Reduction in average response time by 22%: The desktop app allowed users to manage their chats more efficiently, reducing the time customers spent waiting for assistance, and serving more shoppers simultaneously, leading to increased online sales.

•   Improved engagement by 25% in DAU: We saw increased usage of the desktop app, with more frequent logins and longer active sessions compared to the mobile app.
