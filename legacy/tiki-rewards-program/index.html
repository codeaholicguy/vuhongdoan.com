Tiki - Rewards Program

Offering users cash reward and various choices of point redemption

Platform
Mobile App​​​​​​​
Year
Late 2021
Team
PM, Engineers, Illustrator, Designer

My Role
Product Designer

Background
Tiki is one of Vietnam's most popular eCommerce platforms, with more than 6M active users in 2021. From our data, we recognized that our user retention rate is falling as the market has recently become more competitive. We held multiple brainstorming sessions to determine what we could do to improve our customer retention and their loyalty.

As a result, we decided to build a tokenized point-based reward program with easy-to-earn points that can be used in a variety of ways.
Challenge
• Not all users are created equal, depends on loyalty and profitability. How might we design a reward program that can fit all users?
• The concept of the tokenized point is new to the market. How might we help users see the potential value of reward points?
Discover
The research to validate the business proposition was done by the board team before I stepped in. So I started by reviewing some market research in this field and conducted secondary research to learn about: what applications are already on the market, what kind of value propositions they highlighted, and what market demand prospects are being overlooked. Together with PM and a product analyst, we looked up our data to have a deeper understanding of how loyal and profitable our users are.
What we learned
Many apps are focused on providing innumerable offers for users to redeem their reward points, but only a few of them are related to user needs.
Opportunity: Personalize our offers; increase the number of uses for our reward points, such as utilizing them to upgrade tiers or play games (with a chance to win higher prizes).

Most rewards points are expirable and have a fixed cash value.
Opportunity: Make our reward points the second currency in our system, and their cash value can rise (or fall) due to changes in market demand and supply

Our users are completely diverse in terms of how much they spend, how often they return, and how long they've been our customers (from data).
Opportunity: Create a tiered rewards program to encourage highly profitable users to shop more regularly and less profitable users to spend more.
Ideation
User Flows & Wireframes
When we were all aligned on the product requirements, I started several options to enhance user flow and experience.
After a few iterations, our team decided to proceed with the concept as follows:
• Create a Rewards Home as a feed page where users can quickly check all the latest updates and understand what features are available in our program
• Highlight the cash value of reward points since it is our key selling point in this program
High-fidelity Design
I moved forward with the process to the hi-fi phase after validating the wireframes. Since roughly 70% of our all visits came from mobile devices, I started by designing the mobile app first, then translated the design into a desktop version.

Tier Details

Redeem Points

Validation
The hi-fi prototype was created to test my ideas and validate the design. I modified the mockups through multiple iterations based on stakeholder feedback before handing them off to development. Then, for a month, we beta-launched it to our internal users to gather feedback and make improvements before releasing it to all users.
Afterthoughts
In the beginning, I was overwhelmed by the idea of building a tokenized point-based reward program. But through the discovery & research phase, I clearly understood our business goals, our user needs, and found a way to balance them through designs.
​
Future improvements
• Provide a brief introduction for first-time users to walk them through our rewards program
• When we have a lot of offers for users to redeem, we should provide a search tool and improve navigation.
